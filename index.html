Bio

I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) at MIT, where I develop machine learning methods for applications in healthcare and biomedicine. My work focuses on developing models that generate clinically useful data—such as radiology reports or biomolecular assays—from more more accessible sources-like histology images and ECGs. I'm supported in part by a fellowship from the Siebel Scholars Foundation.

I’m advised by Collin M. Stultz (MIT RLE, MIT CSAIL, Harvard/MGH) and Jian Shu (Harvard/MGH). Prior to this, I was advised by Aviv Regev (Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, Genentech) as a research intern in her laboratory.

Before graduate school, I was a software engineer at Facebook, working on their AI Fairness team. I’ve also interned at Facebook, Dropbox, and Accenture.

I received my B.S. in Computer Science with Distinction from Yale, completing significant additional coursework in both Music and Statistics.
